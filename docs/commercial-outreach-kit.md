# Warehouse & Commercial Outreach Kit

**Written 2026-07-17.** Purpose: fill 5 technicians' schedules with commercial
work — warehouses, distribution centers, and buildings with roll-up gates and
commercial overhead doors. SEO compounds over months; outbound books jobs this
week. Every claim below is already published on the site — nothing here needs
legal review before sending.

**Who sends this: the owner (or the designated commercial point person).**
The AI can draft/personalize batches of these on request.

---

## 1. Build the target list (2 hours, one time)

Open Google Maps, search each of these in your dispatch areas, and copy
name + address + phone into a sheet (or straight into the CRM):

- "warehouse" / "distribution center" — Maspeth, College Point, JFK corridor
  (Queens); East New York, Sunset Park (Brooklyn); Meadowlands: Secaucus,
  Carlstadt, Moonachie (NJ); Hauppauge Industrial Park, Route 110 corridor (LI)
- "self storage" — every borough + LI (dozens of roll-up doors per site)
- "auto body shop" / "tire shop" — overhead doors that cycle constantly
- "moving company" / "3PL" / "freight" — dock doors + rolling gates
- "property management" — one relationship = many buildings

Prioritize anything within 20 minutes of where a tech is already based.
Target: 100 rows. That's 2-3 weeks of calls at 10/day.

## 3. Follow-up cadence (most replies come here)

- **Day 3 — bump:** "Following up on the note below — should I send our COI
  and rate sheet so we're on file as a backup vendor at minimum?"
  (Being the *backup* vendor is a low-friction yes. Their primary vendor
  fails eventually.)
- **Day 8 — value drop:** send one real photo (the dock-door job) +
  "Recent dock door install for a warehouse account. If you have doors due
  for spring or cable work, the baseline survey is free."
- **Day 15 — close the loop:** "Last note — if door repair is handled, no
  reply needed. If it's ever 6am and a bay won't open: (914) 557-6816,
  we answer."

## 4. Phone script opener (for the same list)

> "Hi — quick question, who handles your overhead door and gate repairs?
> [pause] We're a local commercial door company — the reason I'm calling is
> we offer a free baseline survey where a tech documents the condition of
> every door at the facility, no commitment. Facilities folks use it for
> budgeting even when they have a vendor. Who would be the right person to
> set that up with?"

## 5. What NOT to promise (until owner confirms terms)

- Net-30 billing — not yet published; say "we can discuss terms" if asked.
- A guaranteed response window in minutes/hours — say "priority dispatch
  with an exact ETA when you call" (published language, LSA-safe).
- Named client references — the anchor client stays anonymous. Use the
  dock-door photo and per-door record samples as proof instead.

## 7. Weekly rhythm to keep 5 techs busy

- Mon-Fri: 10 cold calls + 10 emails from the list (owner or point person)
- Each completed commercial job → ask for the review + snap one photo for
  the Drive folder (city in the filename)
- Friday: 15 minutes — mark replies in the CRM, move warm ones to a
  "survey scheduled" column. The baseline survey IS the sales visit.
