# Backlink Policy — Non-Negotiable Rules

These rules protect real revenue-producing domains. No exception without the
owner's explicit, informed approval in writing.

## Forbidden — never, on any site

- PBNs, link farms, link wheels, tiered link schemes
- Bought link "packages" (Fiverr/marketplace links, "DA90 guest post $50" offers)
- Mass AI-generated guest posts; spun/duplicated articles
- Comment spam, forum-signature spam, wiki spam, profile-link dumps
- Fake or irrelevant directories (any directory whose only product is links)
- Hacked/injected links, expired-domain redirects
- Fake reviews or reciprocal review-swapping
- Scholarship-page schemes with no genuine scholarship
- Widgets/badges engineered for embedded keyword links
- Automated outreach blasts (>~15 sends/week per site, or any un-personalized template blast)
- Anything the sender would be embarrassed to explain to the linking site's editor

## Paid / sponsored / partner placements

Allowed ONLY when all of these hold:
1. The placement has standalone business value (real audience, referral potential,
   listing customers actually browse) — we'd buy it even if links didn't exist.
2. `rel="sponsored"` (or nofollow) on anything paid, per Google policy. A paid
   followed link is a policy violation on both ends — don't request it.
3. Logged in the CSV with `Opportunity Type = paid/sponsored`, price in Notes.
4. Owner approved the spend beforehand.
Chamber/association memberships and legitimate trade directories (ThomasNet, IDA,
BBB, Jewelers of America) count as memberships with citation benefits — fine, log
them the same way.

## Outreach ethics

- Truth only: no fake personas, no "I was just browsing and loved your site" lies,
  no invented mutual connections. We write as the actual business/owner.
- Every email offers the recipient something real (a useful asset, a genuine
  partnership, a real testimonial, a fixed broken link).
- One follow-up after ~5–7 days, one more after ~14. Then stop — mark `no-response`.
- Honor every opt-out immediately and log it.
- **AI models draft; the owner sends** (or explicitly approves sending). No
  autonomous outreach, account creation, directory submission, or payment. Ever.

## Anchor text rules

Natural profiles only. Target mix across each site's earned links:
- ~40–50% brand ("Smart Garage Doors", "Zack & Elle", "Zack & Elle Diamonds")
- ~15–20% URL/naked (smartestgaragedoors.com)
- ~20–30% natural descriptive phrases ("a garage door repair company in Queens",
  "this NYC lab-grown diamond jeweler", "their spring-replacement cost guide")
- ≤10% partial-match; **near-zero exact-match money anchors** ("garage door repair
  queens" as anchor = red flag)
- We *suggest* anchors in outreach at most once and never insist — editors choosing
  their own words is the healthiest signal there is.

## Quality scoring (score every prospect before outreach)

Each 1–5 in the CSV:
- **Relevance** — topical/geographic fit (property-manager page for SGD = 5;
  generic "home tips" blog = 2)
- **Customer value** — could a real buyer arrive through this link?
- **Trust/Authority** — is the site real, maintained, cited by others? (Judgment +
  visible signals until we have tool data; don't fabricate DA numbers)
- **Difficulty** — 1 = submit a form, 5 = needs a relationship or PR win
- **Risk** — 1 = clean (chamber, supplier), 5 = gray (auto-reject at 4+)
- **Priority** = Relevance + Customer value + Trust − Difficulty − Risk (work the
  CSV sorted by this)

**The standing tiebreak:** a Relevance-5/Authority-2 local vendor page beats a
Relevance-2/Authority-5 blog. Customers over metrics.
