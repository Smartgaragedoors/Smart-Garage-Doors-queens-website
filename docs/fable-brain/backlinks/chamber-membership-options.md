# Chamber of Commerce Membership Options — Draft for Owner Review

**Status: research only. Nothing submitted, no accounts created, no payments made.**

Why this matters: competitor research (`competitor-backlink-map.md`) found that
Overhead Door Co. of the Meadowlands is listed on its regional chamber's
directory (`local.meadowlands.org`) — a real backlink + local-trust signal you
don't currently have. Chamber membership is a normal paid business relationship
(not a fake credential), and gets you a directory listing + local legitimacy.

Pick based on where you actually want more commercial visibility — you don't
need all of these, and cost/value differs a lot by chamber.

| Chamber | Cost (as published) | What you get | Fit for you |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Greater Flushing Chamber of Commerce** (flushingchamber.nyc) | $120/yr (1–5 employees) | Directory listing, local advocacy, multilingual community ties (English/Chinese/Korean) | Best fit — your primary Flushing address is literally in their coverage area, and it's the cheapest option here |
| **Queens Chamber of Commerce** (queenschamber.org) | $370/yr (Small Business Partner tier) or $4,800/yr (Corporate) | Online + print membership directory (800+ orgs), event access, advocacy | Good fit — broader Queens-wide visibility than the Flushing-only chamber, worth it if you want borough-wide reach |
| **Business Council of Westchester** (thebcw.org) | Not published — tiered ("Introductory" for small business up to "Concierge"); contact them for the small-business rate | Searchable online directory, 1,000+ member network | Fits your Westchester/Suffern-hub coverage — relevant since Ben's hub serves this area |
| **Bergen County Chamber of Commerce** (bergencountychamber.com) | Not published — has a "subscription plan" page, need to contact directly | Directory + networking events | Fits your NJ commercial coverage (Bergen County is in your named service area) — lowest priority of the four since pricing isn't even public yet |

## Recommendation

Start with **Greater Flushing** (cheapest, exact-match location) and **Queens
Chamber** (broader reach, still your home borough) — both have clear published
pricing and a real directory. Hold off on Westchester/Bergen until you've seen
whether the first two actually move the needle, consistent with "prove it
before scaling" approach used elsewhere in this plan.
