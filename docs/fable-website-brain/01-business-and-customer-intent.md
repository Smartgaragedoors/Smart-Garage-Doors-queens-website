# Business & Customer Intent

Two very different visitors arrive here. Write for the right one.

## Visitor A: the homeowner in a broken-door emergency (most traffic)

They're stressed, on a phone, car possibly trapped. They don't read — they scan for:
does this company fix MY problem, near ME, can I reach a human NOW. That's why the
homepage leads with "Garage door stuck? We answer 24/7," a `CommonProblems`
symptom-link strip, and a phone CTA before anything else.

| Intent | Worried about | Page must answer | CTA | Trust signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Door won't open / stuck | Car trapped, late for work | "We fix this today" + symptom named | Call (primary) | 24/7 live answer, same-day |
| Spring broke (loud bang) | Danger, cost | It's a known fix, price range ($175–$350 published) | Call | Upfront price before work |
| Cable snapped / off-track | Door falling, safety | Don't touch it; we secure it | Call | Emergency line |
| Opener dead / remote dead | Is it cheap or a new unit? | Repair-vs-replace honestly | Call or WhatsApp photos | Brands serviced (LiftMaster, Chamberlain, Genie…) |
| Noisy door | Annoyance, not urgent | Maintenance/tune-up framing | Book online | Reviews |
| **Needs new door/opener** | Price, being upsold | Cost-guide pages with real installed ranges | Complimentary estimate / WhatsApp photos | Financing (0% promo), 1-yr warranty, brands |
| **Comparing companies** | Getting ripped off | /vs-precision, /vs-overhead-door, /second-opinion, /local-vs-national | Second-opinion CTA (free) | 486+ Google reviews, licensed & insured |
| **Wants a price** | Phone-call pressure | Published ranges on service/cost pages; exact price confirmed upfront | Photo estimate (WhatsApp) | "No surprises" language |
| Same-day urgency | Will anyone come TODAY | "Call for exact ETA" — never promise arrival minutes (LSA compliance) | Call | Real person answers |

**LSA compliance rule (recurring audit theme):** the business's own copy must never
promise arrival times or response minutes ("30-minute response", "there in 90
minutes"). Say "call for an exact ETA", "same-day slots often available." Customer
review quotes mentioning times are fine — those are the customer's words.

## Visitor B: the commercial decision-maker (owner's growth priority)

Property managers, facility/warehouse managers, supers. They research before calling;
they need vendor-qualification proof, not urgency theater.

| Intent | Worried about | Page | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Down bay door stopping trucks | Throughput loss | /loading-dock-door-repair/ | 24/7 priority dispatch call + WhatsApp photos |
| Storefront gate stuck | Security/lost sales | /rolling-steel-gate-repair/ | Emergency call |
| Needs a door vendor for portfolio | COI, invoicing, reliability | /property-managers/ | **CommercialLeadForm** ("Set Up a Vendor Account") — they want a callback, not a cold call |
| Stop running doors to failure | Budget surprises | /commercial-maintenance-contracts/ | Baseline survey |
| On-site staff spots an issue | Doesn't have our number | /report/ (QR placard page) | 3-field report form |

Commercial pages carry the `showCommercialLeadForm` prop on GuidePageTemplate —
company/portfolio-aware form, because the residential book-now form reads as "book my
house call." Anchor account context (never name the client publicly — say "a national
logistics company with facilities in two states"): see the commercial plan doc.

## What NOT to overcomplicate

Emergency visitors get: problem named → phone number → proof → done. Don't add
comparison tables, long explainers, or multi-step forms to emergency paths. Depth
belongs on cost guides, comparison pages, and commercial pages where visitors are in
research mode.
